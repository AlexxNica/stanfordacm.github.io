About

Known for its technical talent and problem solvers, ACM is Stanford’s premier computer science organization. We are a community of passionate students who build exciting projects to tackle challenging technical and social problems. Over time, self-starting individuals have created an enduring culture evidenced by a wide-range of ACM projects in recent years -- most notably YouTube Instant, the predecessor to YouTube’s current autocomplete feature, as well as the technology behind acquired startups Fancy That (Palantir, 2015) and PeerCDN (Yahoo, 2013).

Our mission is to share this culture with the broader Stanford community. In doing so, we are excited to host a variety of initiatives this year.

ACM.IO Speaker Series
The ACM.IO speaker series exposes a highly technical audience of computer science and engineering students to emerging technologies used in industry. Past speaker series, which included presentations by LinkedIn, Microsoft, Pinterest, and Stripe, experienced incredible success with high student engagement. Through ACM.IO, we invite companies to showcase their most interesting technical solutions as a means of engaging top computer science and engineering talent.

Enigma Puzzlehunt
Engima Puzzlehunt is a timed problem-solving challenge geared towards students interested in puzzle-solving and computer science. Over the course of a day, students will form groups and solve logic puzzles, brain teasers, and coding challenges for prizes. We designed Puzzlehunt to be a fun yet competitive event for students to showcase their practical skills under pressure.

ACM Exclusive Events
To leverage our diverse and connected network of alumni and members, ACM regularly hosts alumni and member mixers. ACM alumni include the founders of Fancy That, PeerCDN, Affinity, and Kofa as well as engineers at many successful Silicon Valley technology firms.

Get in Touch!

The Stanford chapter of the ACM currently has 1000+ members. We serve the Stanford community by organizing numerous industry, academic, and social events each quarter.

To get in touch, send us an email at acm-officers@lists.stanford.edu and we'll follow up! Or simply join our mailing list.
